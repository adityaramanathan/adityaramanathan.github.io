Project on Reducing Residential Energy Consumption

A guide to making your home more energy-efficient and saving money.

Project Overview

This website presents the findings of my high school project, which explores practical and affordable ways for households to lower their energy use. Through research and data analysis, I've identified several key areas where significant reductions can be made. This project aims to educate and empower people to make smarter choices for their wallets and the environment.

Key Findings

  • Lighting is a Major Factor

    Replacing traditional incandescent bulbs with energy-efficient LED bulbs can reduce lighting-related energy use by up to 80%.

  • Vampire Power is Real

    Electronics and appliances consume energy even when turned off. Unplugging devices or using a smart power strip can significantly reduce this "vampire" or phantom load.

  • HVAC System Maintenance

    Ensuring heating and cooling systems are well-maintained and that homes are properly insulated can drastically reduce energy used for temperature control.

Actionable Tips for Your Home

  1. Switch to LED bulbs throughout your home.
  2. Unplug chargers and appliances when not in use.
  3. Adjust your thermostat a few degrees cooler in winter and warmer in summer.
  4. Seal any drafts around windows and doors.
  5. Wash clothes in cold water whenever possible.
  6. Air-dry laundry on a line or drying rack.
